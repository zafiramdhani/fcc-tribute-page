Prof. DR. Ing. H. Bacharuddin Jusuf Habibie

The Indonesia's "Father of Airplane"

    Timeline :

  • 1936 : Born in Parepare, South Sulawesi, Indonesia
  • 1960 : Graduated from Institute of Technology of North Rhine–Westphalia in Aachen, West Germany and received an engineer's degree in Germany with the title Diplom-Ingenieur.
  • 1962 : Habibie returned to Indonesia for three months on sick leave and married with Hasri Ainun, the daughter of R. Mohamad Besari.
  • 1963 : They had a son, named Ilham Akbar Habibie.
  • 1965 : Habibie delivered his dissertation in aerospace engineering and received the grade of "very good," giving him the title Doktoringenieur (Dr.-Ing.).
  • 1970 : Habibie did accept a position with Messerschmitt-Bölkow-Blohm in Hamburg. There, he developed theories on thermodynamics, construction, and aerodynamics known as the Habibie Factor, Habibie Theorem, and Habibie Method, respectively.
  • 1974 : He was promoted to vice president of the company.
  • 1976 : Habibie was made CEO of the new state-owned enterprise Industri Pesawat Terbang Nurtanio (IPTN; Nurtanio Aircraft Industry).
  • 1980 : IPTN had grown considerably, specializing in the manufacture of helicopters and small passenger planes.
  • 1995 : Under Habibie's leadership, IPTN became a manufacturer of aircraft including Puma helicopters and CASA planes. It pioneered a small passenger airplane, the N-250 Gatotkaca, but theh project was a commercial failure.
  • 2000 : IPTN was changed to PT. Dirgantara Indonesia (Indonesian Aerospace).
  • 2006 : he released a book called Detik-Detik Yang Menentukan: Jalan Panjang Indonesia Menuju Demokrasi (Decisive Moments: Indonesia's Long Road Towards Democracy).
  • 2019 : Dies at age of 83 because of heart problems.
